About Me


Hi I'm Jared. I'm currently a project/product manager at Pakpobox, where we try to make the world a better place by putting your parcels into metal boxes.

I'm originally from Sydney and have been in Hong Kong for 3 years now. I'm a trained civil engineer, with a strong interest in coastal management. I still love the beach, and look to clean them up whenever I can, but made the move into IT project management after coming to Hong Kong.

I have lots of hobbies (I can't really focus on any one thing), including hiking, travelling (which will become really obvious on the other page), and photography. I try to combine all three things, but the weekends I am in Hong Kong I can be found on the trails. I'm also heavily interested in music and dabble with guitar (I enjoy it. I'm not Van Halen.).

I've just started learning front-end web development with Xccelerate, which I'm enjoying a lot. I really want to understand how the development team I work with at Pakpobox thinks, and what difficulties they encounter in their day-to-day work. After completing the front-end web course, I'm hoping to move onto learning some programming languages better suited for backend work.. and maybe one day start my own company and develop my own product.

Travel

I absolutely love to get out of Hong Kong! When I can fly and I have a long weekend, I am usually somwewhere else in Asia. Coming from Australia, it was pretty hard to get out; the closest country larger than Tasmania (Indonesia in case you were curious) which isn't New Zealand is 8 hours away by plane! I'm not particularly fussed on where I go, but once COVID-19 is over I'm planning on going on a few treks in Central Asia and in Africa. To the right are a few of my better or more memorable photos- I really need to remember to take more!

In 2017 I went to Nepal over the Christmas break. I travelled around the Kathmandu Basin before meeting up with my sister, who went on to Everest base Camp. This photo was taken in the middle of me getting scammed.

The first of my many trips in 2018 was to Taiwan, where I managed to see the Pingxi Lantern festival. The square where they released the lanterns was so crowded, but it was definitely worth it to get there early and have a front-row seat!

I head to Japan fairly often (when we can fly), but this was a special trip with my parents. We spent a day in the Mt Fuji region, where I walked them to exhaustion. I think they thought it was worth it when they saw Mt Fuji, though.

I love ruins, so I definitely had to go to Angkor in Cambodia. If you're lucky sunrise can be amazing, but you can nearly guarantee that there will be half of Siem Reap with you. The Angkor complex is so huge I spent a whole day there - I didn't eve see half of it!

When one of my mates told me he was moving to Shanghai, we agreed that we'd have to visit each others cities. Well only half of that happened. It was really fun and I want to go again; it feels completely different to the Guangdong province, which I'm more familiar with.

In 2019 I climbed Mt Fuji's Yoshida Trail. It was so foggy I actually never saw the summit until I stood on it! While climbing I met quite a few people from all over the world and has made me keen to climb more mountains!

In 2020 I was lucky enough to travel all over Sri Lanka. The landscapes are amazing, with really diverse climates, from the tropical southern regions to the more temperate center.
